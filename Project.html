Thermoelectric generators (TEGs) are innovative devices that convert waste heat into electrical energy, offering a significant boost to energy efficiency. They operate based on the Seebeck effect, which generates voltage when two different conductive materials are joined and subjected to a temperature difference. In a TEG, one side is heated while the other remains cool, causing charge carriers—electrons in n-type materials and holes in p-type materials—to move toward the cooler side. This movement generates a current that can be harnessed for practical use. TEGs are compact, solid-state devices with no moving parts, making them highly reliable and low-maintenance. They can be used in various applications, from powering small electronic devices to providing energy in remote locations or waste heat recovery systems. These generators find applications in various sectors, including waste heat recovery in industries, powering remote locations, and cooling systems, leveraging the unique ability to operate in reverse through the Peltier effect. While TEGs offer high reliability and scalability, challenges remain, such as relatively low efficiency and high material costs, which hinder broader adoption.
How to setup a TEG module (Matrials-Required) :-
  1. Get a TEG module from any source
  2. Get two small Heat Sinks
  3. Get a Thermal Paste
  4. Get a small fan
  5. Get a battery
  6. Get a candel
  7. Get a LED or multimeter (Load)
How to setup a TEG module (Setup) :-
  1. Apply thermal paste on the TEG module
  2. Place one heat sink on the TEG module
  3. Place the other heat sink on the other side of the TEG module
  4. Connect the fan to the battery
  5. And place it on the top of the heat sink
  6. Then on the lower heat sink light a candle
  7. Connect the load to the TEG module
